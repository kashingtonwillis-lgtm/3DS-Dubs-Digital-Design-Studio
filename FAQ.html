What does your end-to-end design process look like?

  1. Identify the Problem: To first identify the problem that needs to be solved, I conduct thorough research into the history and existing conditions through immersion in the subject matter.
  2. Explore Perspectives: Secondly, I explore multiple perspectives, define user needs, and question existing thinking about the opportunities and challenges.
  3. Create with Vision: Then, by having a clear vision to create, and considering that many possible solutions exist, I am able to refine the most promising concepts.
  4. Craft Captivating Designs: The challenge lies in crafting captivating designs, and then choosing the best solution by implementing it effectively. This comprehensive approach allows me to tackle complex problems, address user needs, and deliver innovative solutions that truly elevate the final outcome.

Clients often want to know how you move from a concept to a final product. A typical workflow involves a Discovery phase to understand goals and target audiences, followed by Strategy and Design (where UX/UI and graphic design take place), and finally a Production or Deployment phase for video editing and social media asset delivery.

What specific deliverables will I receive at the end of the project?

Since your services range from static graphics to video, clients will need to know the final file formats. This might include high-fidelity prototypes for UX/UI (like Figma or Sketch files), vector files for graphic design (AI, EPS, PDF), and optimized MP4 or MOV files for social media video content.

How do you ensure brand consistency across different types of content?

With services spanning social media, video, and UI, it’s crucial to explain how you maintain a unified brand identity. You can highlight that you use shared style guides or design systems to ensure the visual language of your social media posts matches the user interface of your digital products.
